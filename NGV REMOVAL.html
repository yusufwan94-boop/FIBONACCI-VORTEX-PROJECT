Zone 1: The Stator Wake Shedding Effect

In a traditional engine, placing stationary metal blades directly in the path of hot gas creates high shear layers. These layers break off into spinning eddies (von Kármán wake vortices) that violently slap the spinning rotor, creating noise, structural rattle, and high drag.

These wakes cause a fluctuating boundary layer on the rotor blades, reducing lift and accelerating metal fatigue.
